## Project Five – HR Employee Attrition Dashboard  
[GitHub Repository](https://github.com/Temitopeadep/HR-EMPLOYEE-ATTRITION-DASHBOARD)  
[Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/temitope.adepoju/viz/HREMPLOYEEATTRITIONDASHBOARD/PAGE1)  

### Overview  
Provides insights into employee attrition patterns to help HR teams improve workforce retention strategies.  

### Data Source  
- Meriskill  

### Tools  
- Excel – Data cleaning and transformation  
- Tableau – Analysis and visualization  

### Data Cleaning Steps  
1. Removed redundant and duplicate columns  
2. Renamed columns for clarity  
3. Dropped duplicate records  
4. Cleaned inconsistent entries  
5. Removed missing values  
6. Transformed data for analysis readiness  

### Analysis Areas  
- **Demographics**: workforce distribution by age, gender, marital status, and distance from home  
- **Turnover Analysis I**: attrition by department, role, business travel, and tenure  
- **Turnover Analysis II**: attrition by job level, overtime, performance rating, and monthly income  
- **Employee Wellness**: job involvement, satisfaction, work-life balance, and environmental fit  

### Key Results  
- Total employees: 1,470  
- Total attrition: 237 employees  
- Single employees had the highest attrition (61%)  
- Male employees recorded higher attrition numbers  
- 84.3% of employees who left had low performance ratings  
- Entry-level employees were most affected

## Project Six – Titanic Survival Analysis  
[GitHub Repository](https://github.com/Temitopeadep/TITANIC-DATASET)  

### Overview  
This project uses **Python and pandas** to analyze survival patterns in the Titanic dataset, uncovering key demographic and socio-economic factors influencing survival.  

### Objectives  
- Identify survival trends based on sex, class, and embarkation point  
- Strengthen data wrangling and visualization skills  
- Practice exploratory data analysis (EDA)  

### Tools & Libraries  
- Python  
- pandas – data manipulation  
- matplotlib & seaborn – data visualization  

### Key Insights  
- **Gender**: Women had higher survival rates than men  
- **Overall Survival Rate**: 38% of passengers survived  
- **Passenger Class**: First-class passengers had the highest survival, third-class the lowest  
- **Embarkation**: Most passengers boarded at Southampton
